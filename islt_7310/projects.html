Trenton High School

High School English Teacher, August 2021-present

In my current position, I teach five sections of 11th grade English language arts. In this course, students read and analyze the standards of American literature The Crucible, The Great Gatsby, the poetry of Whitman and Hughes, the short stories of Hawthorne and Poe, seminal speeches and documents). I also teach research skills, including using databases, paraphrasing and embedding quotes, and correctly citing sources.
Throughout the 2021-2022 school year, the faculty at Trenton High School has been working on aligning the curriculum with Missouri Learning Standards. I worked with the English department to identify priority standards for 11th grade. I am currently writing success criteria for each priority standard. As the sole LA III teacher at THS, I have great flexibility when it comes to curriculum. I have used this flexibility to prioritize authentic, in-depth learning over covering the maximum amount of content. These experiences include the class blog discussed here.

I adapted this project from a more traditional culminating project where students create a newspaper at the end of a unit. In this project, students write blog posts over events in the novel throughout the unit. This allows students to share their analysis beyond the confines of the classroom.

Moberly High School

High School English, August 2020-May 2021

At Moberly High School, I taught four sections of freshman LA I and three sections of junior LA III. In this position, I worked closely with the other English faculty members to plan units. Due to the Covid-19 pandemic, the school offered online sections of each course during the 2020-2021 school year. I taught one online section of LA I and one online section of LA III. I utilized many technologies to deliver content to my online students. I used Edpuzzles to present content, screencasts to explain assignments, and Google Meet to communicate face-to-face. These digital lessons and tools were also handy for keeping in-person students on track during the frequent quarantines that took place during the year.
Moberly is the largest district at which I have taught. This had positive and challenging aspects. One positive aspect was that I worked with a more racially diverse population than I have taught in other schools. This meant that students brought more diverse backgrounds and opinions to the table when reading texts like To Kill a Mockingbird. The main challenge in this position was the lack of flexibility in planning the curriculum. Because multiple teachers taught the same course, we were expected to be on the same page.

This video was one of the best resources I created for my online students. I created a digital classroom on Google Slides, then added a digital version of myself via Bitmoji. For each slide, I wrote content in a textbox and put that on the whiteboard. I then recorded a lesson via Screencastify and uploaded the video to Youtube.

Tri-County High School

5th-8th Grade English, August 2017-May 2020

At Tri-County (Jamesport), I taught a variety of middle school English classes. The exact classes varied each year. My first year, I taught 6th, 7th, and 8th grade English and a creative writing exploratory class. The next year, I taught 5th, 6th, and 7th grade reading and writing. My final year, I taught 5th, 6th, and 7th grade English and a drama exploratory. I was instrumental in introducing Google Workspace for Education at the middle school. Having Google accounts opened doors for students to create more content using technology. In addition to my classroom responsibilities, I also directed the middle school play for two years. During my final year in this position, the district began the process of revamping the curriculum to better align with state standards. As part of this goal, I rewrote several novel units to address specific literature standards.
Jamesport Tri-County is a rural district where teachers have smaller classes and more preps than at larger districts. Planning for multiple preps was a challenge. However, I started this position earlier in my teaching career and enjoyed the opportunity to put into action the strategies I learned during college. At first I tried to use a reading and writing workshop model. I later switched to whole class literature units, such as The Outsiders for 7th graders. In all grade levels, I emphasized writing. Students wrote fiction, research papers, and procedural writing.

One of the units I taught in 6th grade reading was over the novel Surviving the Applewhites. This novel is perfect for middle school reading because it shows such clear examples of character development, theme, and even symbolism. After teaching the book several times using ready-made materials, I decided to create my own unit from scratch, including a webquest, chapter worksheets, and a culminating project.
